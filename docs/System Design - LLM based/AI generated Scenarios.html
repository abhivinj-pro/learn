# Advanced RAG and Agent Systems: Scenario Question Bank

# Scenario 1 - Enterprise Knowledge Base RAG at Scale

You are building a RAG system for a 50,000-employee company. The corpus is 10M+ internal documents: PDFs, wikis, Slack exports, Confluence pages, Jira tickets, and email threads. The system must handle 100k+ queries/day.

  • How do you design the ingestion pipeline to handle heterogeneous document formats at this scale? Walk me through the architecture end-to-end.
  • Your PDF ingestion is failing silently on scanned documents and complex table layouts. How do you detect this, and what’s your remediation strategy?
  • How do you handle documents that are updated frequently vs. documents that are immutable? What does your index freshness strategy look like?
  • Confluence pages contain embedded Jira tickets, which contain comments, which link to Slack threads. How do you model these relationships, and does your chunking strategy change?
  • You have documents in 18 languages. How does this affect your embedding model choice, chunking, and retrieval strategy?
  • A senior executive asks: “Why did the system answer X when our internal policy clearly says Y?” How do you build a system that lets you audit and explain a specific retrieval+generation decision?
  • How do you handle access control? A user should only see documents they have permission to view. Where in the pipeline do you enforce this - at retrieval time, re-ranking, or generation?
  • Your embedding model vendor (say, OpenAI) deprecates the model you used to embed all 10M documents. Walk me through your re-embedding migration strategy with zero downtime.
  • How do you measure whether your RAG system is actually better than the old keyword search employees were using?
  • After 6 months in production, users complain the system “feels stale.” How do you diagnose whether this is a data freshness problem, an embedding drift problem, or a user expectation problem?

# Scenario 2 - Multi-Tenant RAG SaaS Platform

You are building a RAG platform that will be sold to thousands of companies. Each tenant uploads their own documents and each has isolated retrieval requirements. You need to serve all tenants from shared infrastructure.

  • How do you architect tenant isolation in your vector database? What are the trade-offs between namespace-based isolation, collection-based isolation, and fully separate vector DB instances per tenant?
  • A new enterprise tenant has 500GB of documents and needs to be fully operational within 4 hours of signing. How do you architect the ingestion pipeline to meet this SLA?
  • A small tenant’s queries are starting to slow down because a large tenant is hammering the vector DB. How did you design for this, and what’s your mitigation?
  • How do you handle schema-level customization - a legal firm wants to chunk by clause, a software company wants to chunk by function - while keeping a shared infrastructure?
  • How do you do cross-tenant evaluation? You want to measure retrieval quality across tenants without exposing one tenant’s data to another.
  • A tenant wants to bring their own embedding model. How does this affect your architecture?
  • How do you design cost allocation and chargeback - embedding, storage, and inference all have different cost profiles per tenant?
  • Tenant A wants RAG with their own fine-tuned LLM. Tenant B wants GPT-4. How does your serving layer handle this?
  • How do you design the metadata schema to allow per-tenant custom filters without requiring schema migrations every time a new tenant onboards?
  • How do you handle tenant offboarding, ensuring their data is provably deleted across embeddings, chunks, source documents, and logs?

# Scenario 3 - RAG Over Structured + Unstructured Data

Your system needs to answer questions that require joining information from a SQL database (product catalog, pricing, inventory), unstructured PDFs (product manuals, compliance docs), and semi-structured JSON logs (user behavior data).

  • How do you architect a hybrid retrieval system that can decide whether to hit a vector store, SQL, or an API at query time?
  • A user asks: “Which of our products comply with EU regulation X and are currently in stock in Germany?” Walk me through how this query gets routed and answered.
  • When you retrieve a SQL table row and a PDF chunk as context, how do you handle the token budget? What do you include, what do you truncate?
  • The LLM generates SQL to query your database as part of a Text-to-SQL approach. How do you prevent SQL injection, runaway queries, or data exfiltration?
  • How do you handle temporal joins - the user is asking about pricing as it was on a specific past date, but your database only keeps current values?
  • The product catalog has 2M SKUs. Embedding them all is expensive and may not produce meaningful semantic similarity. How do you decide what to embed vs. what to hit with structured filters?
  • How do you evaluate whether the structured retrieval path or unstructured retrieval path is the bottleneck when a query returns a wrong answer?
  • How do you design fallback logic when the structured query returns no results but the unstructured search does, or vice versa?
  • A compliance team auditor asks you to show a log of every SQL query the system ran on their behalf over the past 90 days. How is this captured in your architecture?
  • How do you handle numerical reasoning? The LLM needs to compare pricing across 30 SKUs but can’t reliably do arithmetic. What’s your strategy?

# Scenario 4 - High-Throughput, Low-Latency RAG in Production

You need to serve a RAG system at p99 latency under 2 seconds for 500 concurrent users, with no degradation under load spikes of 10x.

  • Walk me through every latency component in a RAG pipeline and how you’d budget them to stay under 2 seconds at p99.
  • When do you use approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) search vs. exact search, and what does the quality trade-off look like in practice?
  • How do you design a caching strategy for RAG? What’s cacheable (embeddings, retrievals, full responses), and what are the cache invalidation challenges for each?
  • Describe how you’d implement speculative retrieval or prefetching to reduce perceived latency for common query patterns.
  • Your vector DB is the bottleneck. Walk me through your options - sharding, read replicas, query batching, changing index types - and how you choose.
  • How do you handle the cold start problem - a new deployment has empty caches and a hot traffic pattern starting in 30 seconds?
  • A 10x load spike hits. What degrades first in your system, and how do you design graceful degradation instead of hard failure?
  • How do you set top-k? What’s the trade-off between retrieval recall and context size, and how does this interact with your latency budget?
  • How do you implement streaming responses for RAG in a way that feels responsive to users even when the full context assembly is slow?
  • How do you load test a RAG system? Standard HTTP load tests don’t capture embedding inference time, vector search degradation, or LLM backpressure. What does your load testing setup look like?

# Scenario 5 - RAG Evaluation and Quality in Production

You shipped a RAG system. You need to know whether it’s actually working, detect regressions before users do, and drive continuous improvement.

  • What’s the difference between offline evaluation, online evaluation, and shadow evaluation for a RAG system? When do you use each?
  • Walk me through how you’d implement a faithfulness metric - detecting when the LLM’s answer is not grounded in the retrieved context.
  • How do you build a golden evaluation dataset for a domain you just entered, where you don’t have historical Q&A pairs?
  • Context precision and context recall are often in tension. How do you set the operational threshold for your use case?
  • Your RAG system has 92% faithfulness in eval but users are still complaining about hallucinations. What could explain this gap?
  • How do you detect retrieval failures - cases where the relevant document exists in your index but wasn’t retrieved? How do you distinguish this from documents genuinely not being in the corpus?
  • How do you design an LLM-as-judge evaluation pipeline that is itself reliable? What are the known failure modes of LLM judges?
  • How do you instrument your production system to collect signal for continuous improvement without violating user privacy?
  • A business stakeholder says “accuracy went up from 78% to 84%.” How do you explain what that number actually means, what it doesn’t tell you, and what you’d additionally measure?
  • How do you version-control your evaluation pipeline so that as your prompts and retrieval strategy change, you can still compare results across versions?

# Scenario 6 - Agentic RAG / Autonomous Research Agent

You’re building an agent that, given a complex user query, can autonomously decide what to retrieve, from where, in what order, and iterate until it has a confident answer. Think “deep research” functionality.

  • How do you architect the control loop - when does the agent decide it has enough information vs. needs another retrieval step?
  • How do you prevent infinite retrieval loops? What’s your circuit-breaker design?
  • The agent retrieves conflicting information from two authoritative sources. How does it decide what to include in the final answer, and how does it communicate the conflict to the user?
  • How do you design the agent’s query reformulation strategy - when a retrieval returns poor results, how does the agent decide to rephrase vs. use a different source vs. admit it doesn’t know?
  • How do you bound the cost of a single agentic RAG call? A complex query could trigger 50+ retrievals. What’s your budget enforcement mechanism?
  • How do you handle the case where the agent needs to retrieve from a slow external API mid-flow - do you block, parallelize, or cache?
  • What does observability look like for an agentic RAG system? What do you capture in your traces, and how do you debug a wrong answer?
  • The user’s query spans multiple retrieval domains - internal wiki, external web, a customer CRM. How do you design the agent’s tool selection and routing?
  • How do you maintain coherence across multi-turn conversations where each turn triggers a new agentic retrieval chain?
  • How do you evaluate agentic RAG? The intermediate retrieval steps are part of the quality story, not just the final answer.

# Scenario 7 - Multi-Agent Orchestration System

You’re building a multi-agent system where specialized agents (research agent, code agent, data analysis agent, communication agent) collaborate to complete long-horizon tasks.

  • Walk me through an architecture for a multi-agent system where agents can delegate sub-tasks to each other. How do you prevent deadlocks and circular delegation?
  • How do you design the communication protocol between agents - message queues, shared state, direct API calls? What are the trade-offs?
  • When Agent A delegates to Agent B and B returns a bad result, how does Agent A decide whether to retry, escalate, or proceed with a fallback?
  • How do you maintain a shared memory/context across agents that is consistent without becoming a bottleneck?
  • How do you version and deploy individual agents independently without breaking the contracts other agents depend on?
  • What’s your strategy for agent-level rate limiting? One runaway agent shouldn’t be able to exhaust the LLM API budget for the whole system.
  • How do you design the orchestrator - is it itself an LLM, a state machine, or a rules engine? What are the trade-offs of each?
  • How do you trace a multi-agent workflow end-to-end when each agent may call different LLMs, tools, and external APIs?
  • A user wants to cancel a long-running multi-agent job that’s already mid-execution. How do you design clean cancellation and rollback?
  • How do you evaluate a multi-agent system? Individual agent quality doesn’t guarantee system-level quality. What does your evaluation harness look like?

# Scenario 8 - Agent Memory Architecture

You’re building a production agent that needs short-term working memory, long-term episodic memory, and semantic memory about a user/domain over months of interaction.

  • Describe the taxonomy of agent memory types you’d implement: in-context, external short-term, external long-term, semantic, procedural. What’s stored where and why?
  • How do you decide what to persist to long-term memory at the end of a session vs. what to discard?
  • How do you implement memory retrieval at the start of a new session without blowing your context window on irrelevant history?
  • How do you handle memory conflicts - the agent learned X about a user in March but the user’s situation changed by June?
  • How do you design memory for multi-user scenarios where the same agent serves thousands of users, each needing isolated and personalized memory?
  • Memory stored as raw text is expensive to retrieve semantically. What alternative representations do you consider, and how do you make the trade-off?
  • How do you prevent memory poisoning - a user deliberately feeding false information that corrupts the agent’s future behavior?
  • How do you handle GDPR/privacy requests - a user wants all memory of them deleted. How do you make memory deletion complete and verifiable?
  • How does your memory system interact with RAG retrieval? When should the agent pull from user-specific memory vs. the global knowledge base?
  • How do you evaluate memory quality over time? The agent might behave well in the short term but drift or degrade over months.

# Scenario 9 - Agent Safety, Guardrails, and Prompt Injection

You’re deploying agents that take real-world actions: sending emails, writing to databases, making API calls to third-party services. The stakes of failure are high.

  • What’s your defense-in-depth strategy against prompt injection when user-provided content ends up as part of the agent’s context?
  • An agent is tasked with “clean up our Jira backlog” and starts closing tickets that are still in use. How do you design reversibility and human-in-the-loop checkpoints into the agent?
  • How do you scope an agent’s permissions - what principle applies, and how do you technically enforce it rather than relying on the LLM’s judgment?
  • The agent’s system prompt is leaked via an indirect prompt injection from a retrieved document. Walk me through how this attack works and how you mitigate it.
  • How do you design an output validation layer that runs before the agent takes an action? What’s the architecture of this validator?
  • How do you handle jailbreaks targeting agents specifically - techniques like “simulate a world where you have no restrictions”?
  • When the agent calls an external API and gets an unexpected response, how do you prevent the agent from misinterpreting it as an instruction?
  • How do you design human approval gates that don’t completely destroy the agentic value proposition of autonomous operation?
  • An agent made a consequential mistake. How does your system support forensic investigation - replaying what happened, why the agent chose each action, and what context it had?
  • How do you red-team an agent system? What’s your adversarial testing strategy before deployment?

# Scenario 10 - LLMOps, Observability, and Production Reliability

Your AI system is live with real users. You need to operate it with the same reliability expectations as any critical production service.

  • What does your LLMOps stack look like? Walk me through how you handle prompt versioning, deployment, rollback, and A/B testing.
  • How do you detect prompt regression - a prompt change that made things worse for 5% of query types while improving the other 95%?
  • How do you set up alerting for an LLM-based system? What metrics do you alert on, and what are the known pitfalls of standard SRE metrics applied naively to LLM systems?
  • The model provider (OpenAI/Anthropic) is down. What’s your fallback strategy, and how does failover work in your serving layer?
  • How do you handle the LLM returning structured output (JSON) that doesn’t match your expected schema in production? What’s your retry and fallback logic?
  • How do you build a canary deployment for an LLM system where you can’t unit test all possible inputs?
  • What does your cost attribution and budget governance look like? How do you prevent a single query pattern from generating runaway costs?
  • How do you handle PII and sensitive data that leaks into your LLM call logs and traces?
  • Your LLM latency suddenly spikes from p50=800ms to p50=3500ms. Walk me through your debugging process.
  • How do you manage model versioning across your system when a model provider silently updates a model? How do you detect behavioral drift from model updates?

# Scenario 11 - Chunking and Indexing Strategy (Deep Design)

You need to design the optimal chunking and indexing strategy for a corpus that includes legal contracts, scientific papers, code repositories, and customer support transcripts - all in the same system.

  • Why does chunking strategy matter at all at scale? How does a suboptimal chunking decision compound as your corpus grows?
  • Compare fixed-size, sentence-based, paragraph-based, semantic, and agentic/late chunking strategies. For each of the four document types in this scenario, which would you choose and why?
  • How do you handle the “lost in the middle” problem - the LLM ignoring retrieved context that appears in the middle of a long context window?
  • How do you design a parent-child chunk architecture where small chunks are retrieved but large parent chunks are sent to the LLM?
  • Code files have a natural hierarchical structure - repository, file, class, function, line. How does your chunking strategy model this, and how do retrieval queries map to it?
  • Customer support transcripts have a temporal structure - turn-by-turn conversation. How do you preserve conversational coherence in your chunks?
  • How do you handle cross-references - a legal contract that says “as defined in Schedule A”? The chunk containing the reference and the chunk containing the definition may be far apart.
  • How do you enrich chunks with metadata at index time to improve retrieval precision? Walk me through your metadata design for each of the four document types.
  • Your corpus has significant redundancy - the same boilerplate clause appears in 50,000 contracts. How does this affect your index, and do you deduplicate?
  • How do you update a single document in a corpus of 10M documents? What’s your partial re-indexing strategy?

# Scenario 12 - Retrieval Strategy and Re-ranking Deep Dive

Your naive vector similarity search is giving mediocre results. You need to design a production retrieval pipeline that is both highly relevant and fast.

  • Explain the difference between dense retrieval, sparse retrieval (BM25), and hybrid retrieval. For what query types does each dominate, and how do you design the hybrid fusion?
  • When and why would you use a cross-encoder re-ranker vs. a bi-encoder? What’s the latency cost, and how do you fit it into your latency budget?
  • How do you implement query expansion and query reformulation at retrieval time? What are the risks of doing this with an LLM in the critical path?
  • HyDE (Hypothetical Document Embeddings) sometimes dramatically improves retrieval. When does it fail, and what’s the failure mode?
  • How do you design a multi-stage retrieval pipeline - first pass with cheap approximate search, second pass with expensive exact/re-ranked search?
  • How do you handle queries with multiple distinct intents in a single question? Should you decompose, retrieve separately, and merge?
  • How do you prevent popularity bias in retrieval - where frequently accessed documents get retrieved even when they’re not the most relevant?
  • Your retrieval consistently fails on rare domain-specific terms not well-represented in your embedding model. What are your options?
  • How do you implement retrieval with time-decay - recent documents should score higher for certain query types but not for others?
  • When a retrieval returns 0 results vs. low-confidence results, how do you handle these two cases differently in your system?
